BRCA Mutation Pre-Screening:

Am I Eligible for the High-Risk OBSP?

Have you been told by a doctor or genetic counselor that you carry any of the following genes: BRCA1, BRCA2, TP53, or PALB2?
Have you been told by a genetic counselor that your risk for breast cancer is higher due to a close family member carrying a BRCA1, BRCA2, TP53, or PALB2 gene?
Have you been assessed by a genetics clinic (using IBIS or CanRisk tools) as having a 25% or greater lifetime risk for breast cancer based on personal and family history?
Have you had radiation therapy to the chest to treat another cancer before age 30 AND was this at least eight years ago?

You're Eligible



You meet the eligibility criteria for the High Risk Ontario Breast Screening Program and may benefit from BRCA mutation screening. Ask your doctor about BRCA screening at your earliest convenience. Please note that individuals with a personal history of breast cancer and/or breast implants are still eligible for screening and those over the age of 70 are not accepted in the program.

You're Not Eligible



You do not meet the eligibility criteria for the High Risk Ontario Breast Screening Program and are not encouraged to pursue BRCA mutation screening at this time. However, regular breast screening may still be important for you, ask your doctor for further medical advice.
